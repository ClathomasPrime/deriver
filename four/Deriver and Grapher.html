A short tutorial:
  • Entering equations:
    • You can use +, -, *, /, and ^ as "infix" operations.
    • You can use sin(), cos(), tan(), and ln() as "prefix" operations.
    • Infix operations need parentheses only when "order of operations" dictates, while prefix operations always need them.
    • The two non-composite expressions are "numbers" and "x". X is the only independent variable you can enter.
    • Exponentiation is left-associative, i.e. x^x^x = (x^x)^x.
  • Adjusting the view
    • The "center" fields correspond to the point in the center of the display field, i.e. the point that the view focuses on.
    • The "scale" fields correspond the the number of pixels per unit on each axis of the graph.
    • I know, this isn't a very intuitive way to do the display...
